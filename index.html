            Privacy

            This tool is designed to respect your privacy. It runs entirely in your browser. No information about you is sent to any server.

            What is stored on your device

            To help you track your caring journey over time, this tool saves a small amount of anonymous data to your browser’s local storage. This stays on your device only.

            • The stage number and title from each check-in (up to 5 check-ins)
            • The date each check-in was completed
            • Whether the urgent support screen was shown
            • Your action plan progress (which items you have ticked)
            • Your chosen check-in reminder date, if you set one
            • Anonymous usage counts (checks started, checks completed, stages reached)
            • Anonymous feedback (whether you found the tool helpful)

            What is never stored

            • Your name, address, or any contact details
            • Your individual answers to any question
            • Any medical or care information
            • Your IP address or device identifiers
            • Cookies — this tool uses none

            No tracking, no servers

            This tool does not use analytics services, advertising networks, or any third-party scripts. No data is sent to Carers Trust Solihull’s servers or anywhere else. Your data never leaves your browser.

            How to delete your data

            You can delete all stored data at any time from the result screen, under “Your data & privacy”. You can also clear browser local storage directly in your browser settings.

            Closing or refreshing the page does not delete your data. Data is deleted only when you choose to clear it, or when you clear your browser storage manually.

            External links

            This tool contains links to external websites (such as Carers Trust Solihull and NHS services). These links open in a new tab. Once you leave this tool, the privacy policies of those websites apply. This tool has no control over external sites.

            Questions about privacy

            If you have questions about how this tool handles data, contact Carers Trust Solihull.

            Accessibility

            Carers Trust Solihull is committed to making this tool as accessible as possible for all users, including those using assistive technology.

            Our commitment

            We aim to meet the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 at Level AA. This is the standard required for UK public sector websites and the benchmark we follow for this tool.

            What we have implemented

            • Keyboard navigation: all interactive elements are reachable and usable by keyboard alone
            • Skip navigation: a skip link allows keyboard and screen reader users to jump straight to the main content
            • Screen reader support: all screens use ARIA landmarks, labels, and live regions so screen readers announce changes correctly
            • Focus indicators: a clear visible outline is shown on every focused element
            • Colour contrast: text and interactive elements meet WCAG AA contrast ratios
            • Touch targets: all buttons and interactive elements meet the 44×44px minimum touch target size
            • Responsive layout: the tool works on screens from 320px wide upwards
            • No time limits: there are no timeouts or auto-advancing steps
            • Plain English: content is written in plain English, avoiding jargon
            • Print support: the result screen prints cleanly and works with Save as PDF

            Known limitations

            • The tool has not yet been tested with specialist assistive technology users in a formal audit. We plan to conduct user testing with screen reader users before the next major release.
            • Some complex interactive patterns (such as the question radio group and action plan checkboxes) may behave inconsistently across older screen readers. We continue to review these.
            • PDF export relies on the browser’s built-in print dialogue, which varies in accessibility between browsers and operating systems.

            Feedback

            If you encounter any accessibility barriers while using this tool, please contact Carers Trust Solihull. Your feedback helps us improve.

            Technical information

            This tool is built with HTML, CSS, and vanilla JavaScript. It uses no third-party frameworks or libraries. It is designed to work without JavaScript for read-only content, and degrades gracefully on older browsers.

                  Staff & volunteer guide

                  This guide explains how to use the Male Carer Stage Checker in a professional or voluntary capacity — for example, during a carer assessment, a drop-in session, or a first contact call.

                  How to introduce the tool

                  Tell the carer: "This is a short tool that helps us understand where you are in your caring role — there are no right or wrong answers. It takes about 2 minutes."

                  Make clear the tool is anonymous — no personal details are collected, and results are stored on this device only.

                  If the carer prefers, they can answer the questions themselves while you observe, or you can read the questions aloud.

                  Using the tool in a session

                  1. Enable Professional mode on the home screen before starting.
                  2. Work through the 7 questions together. Encourage honest answers — there are no wrong responses.
                  3. Review the stage result with the carer. Use the follow-up prompts to explore the result.
                  4. Select one agreed next step the carer feels ready to try before your next contact.
                  5. Use Session summary to copy or print a record for your own notes.

                  Higher-risk responses

                  If a carer selects an answer that indicates high stress, burnout risk, or safety concerns, a yellow support banner will appear on the result screen.

                  The professional escalation note (visible in professional mode) will prompt you to consider a referral or further assessment using your organisation's escalation pathway.

                  When to escalate

                  Escalate if the carer:
                  • Expresses thoughts of self-harm or harming the person they care for
                  • Describes a situation that may meet safeguarding thresholds
                  • Is in crisis and unable to continue caring safely
                  • Requests urgent help directly

                  For immediate danger, direct the carer to call 999. For safeguarding concerns, follow your organisation's local authority referral process.

                  Safeguarding responsibilities

                  This tool does not replace a formal carer assessment, safeguarding referral, or care plan. It is a starting point for conversation, not a clinical or legal record.

                  All safeguarding obligations remain with the professional or organisation using the tool. Results should not be entered verbatim into case management systems without appropriate context.

                  After the session

                  • Use the session summary copy function to paste key details into your own notes.
                  • The tool does not retain data between sessions by default — each new check-in overwrites the last result.
                  • Encourage the carer to check in again independently — share the tool URL or a QR code.
                  • If the carer’s situation changes significantly, repeat the check before the next planned contact.

                  Safeguarding

                  This page explains how this tool approaches safeguarding, and what it does and does not do.

                  Our approach

                  • This tool does not replace professional judgement about risk or need.
                  • It is designed to encourage carers to seek early support — not to assess or diagnose.
                  • It signposts to appropriate local and national services at every stage.
                  • Any response that indicates an immediate risk of harm directs the user to call 999 before continuing.
                  • Urgent risks must always be escalated through your organisation’s safeguarding pathway.

                  If someone is in immediate danger

                  This tool is not for emergencies. If a carer or the person they care for is in immediate danger, call 999.

                  For non-emergency safeguarding concerns in Solihull, contact Solihull Adult Social Care on 0121 788 4300 or visit solihullcarers.org.

                  What this tool does not do

                  • Does not formally assess risk or determine safeguarding thresholds
                  • Does not replace a carer’s assessment under the Care Act 2014
                  • Does not provide medical, legal, or clinical advice
                  • Does not store personal data or create a record of individual cases
                  • Does not send any information to a server or third party
                  • Does not constitute a care plan or support agreement

                  Responsibilities when using this tool

                  If you are a staff member or volunteer using this tool with a carer:

                  • You remain responsible for following your organisation’s safeguarding procedures.
                  • Results should not be entered verbatim into case management systems without appropriate professional context.
                  • If a carer discloses something that raises a safeguarding concern, act on it directly — do not rely on the tool to capture or record it.

                  For organisations

                  This page is for organisations commissioning, deploying, or using this tool with carers. It covers quality assurance, content governance, KPI alignment, partner responsibilities, and operational considerations.

                  Quality assurance checklist

                  Verify these before each public deployment or content update:

                  • All external support links checked and confirmed active
                  • Local service details accurate for the area of deployment
                  • Language reviewed: plain English, no jargon, accessible to all reading levels
                  • Safeguarding wording consistent with current organisational policy
                  • No clinical diagnoses, medical claims, or legal conclusions made
                  • Action plan items practical, realistic, and appropriate for carers
                  • Stage descriptions reviewed for accuracy and relevance
                  • Content reviewed by someone with lived experience of caring, where possible

                  How this supports service outcomes

                  • Early identification of carers — the tool helps people recognise their caring role at an earlier stage, enabling proactive engagement.
                  • Increased access to support — signposting at every stage connects carers with relevant local and national services they may not have known about.
                  • Improved consistency of conversation — using a structured tool with carers ensures a consistent starting point across all staff and volunteers.
                  • Volunteer and staff contribution — the tool can be used independently by the carer or as a guided conversation aid, multiplying the reach of limited staff time.
                  • Reduced risk of late escalation — stage-appropriate signposting and the urgent support route encourage carers to seek help before reaching crisis.
                  • Anonymous impact data — service-level usage data (stored locally, not transmitted) supports quarterly reporting without collecting personal data.

                  Partner responsibilities

                  If you are deploying or embedding this tool on behalf of your organisation, you are responsible for:

                  • Verifying that all local service links are correct for your area before deployment
                  • Ensuring your safeguarding contact routes are current and accurate within the tool
                  • Reviewing content at the recommended interval (every 6 months, or after any service change)
                  • Briefing staff and volunteers who use the tool with carers — see the Staff & volunteer guide
                  • Keeping a record of your own review dates using the content review log
                  • Not making clinical, legal, or diagnostic claims when presenting results to carers

                  Operational considerations

                  • The tool relies on up-to-date local support links — outdated links reduce its value and may mislead carers.
                  • External services may change, merge, or close; periodic review is essential.
                  • Carers may interpret stage results differently to how they are intended; staff should contextualise results in conversation.
                  • The tool does not account for cultural, linguistic, or accessibility factors without local adaptation.

                  Mitigations: regular content review, clear plain-English wording, strong signposting to human support, and staff training on using the tool appropriately.
