Overview

What the tool does — and what it does not

CareStage is a self-serve or guided tool for unpaid male carers. It asks 7 short questions, computes a stage score, and renders a personalised result with stage-appropriate content, resources, and support signposting.

✓ This tool does

  • Help carers identify which of 6 caring stages they are at
  • Surface condition-specific "what may happen next" guidance
  • Provide stage-appropriate action plans, help links, and service suggestions
  • Offer a pressure check and emotional validation section
  • Support professional/volunteer-led conversations with structured prompts
  • Provide an embeddable, brandable experience for partner organisations
  • Work fully offline after first visit (Service Worker, cache-first)
  • Store only anonymous, device-local data — nothing is transmitted

✕ This tool does not

  • ✕Formally assess clinical risk or safeguarding thresholds
  • ✕Replace a carer's assessment under the Care Act 2014
  • ✕Provide medical, legal, or diagnostic advice
  • ✕Make or record referrals to services
  • ✕Store names, contact details, or individual answers
  • ✕Send any data to a server or third party
  • ✕Replace emergency services (999 is always first)

The 6 Stages

What each stage means

Stages are computed from the total score across all 7 questions. Stage 6 is also reachable via the urgent support trigger, regardless of score. Results are snapshots — carers move between stages as circumstances change.

Stage 1 · 0–5 pts Stage 2 · 6–10 pts Stage 3 · 11–15 pts Stage 4 · 16–20 pts Stage 5 · 21–25 pts Stage 6 · 26–34 pts
1
Just Found Out
Score 0–5 · Max possible 34

At the very start of the journey. The situation may feel new, uncertain, or hard to make sense of. The carer may not yet see themselves as a carer, or the diagnosis may still be unclear.

⏱ Check again in 1 to 2 weeks
2
Getting Your Bearings
Score 6–10

Beginning to understand what is happening but still uncertain about next steps. Starting to accept the carer role, but it can feel unfamiliar or a lot to take on at times.

⏱ Check again in 2 to 4 weeks
3
Becoming the Carer
Score 11–15

Settling into the role but it is taking real effort. Tasks are building up and the carer may be managing a lot on their own. Own needs can start to get pushed aside.

⏱ Check again in 4 weeks
4
Building a Routine
Score 16–20

A routine is in place but requires constant effort to maintain. Managing well day to day, but longer-term pressure is building. Strain may be felt personally even if things look organised.

⏱ Check again in 4 to 6 weeks
5
Keeping Going
Score 21–25

Managing, but it is hard. The caring role may feel relentless and the carer's own wellbeing may be suffering. One of the most important stages to access support — before reaching breaking point.

⏱ Check again in 1 to 2 weeks
6
Review, Crisis, or Change
Score 26–34 · or urgent trigger

Something significant is happening. This could be a health crisis, a major change in the caring situation, or a point where the current arrangement is no longer working. Needs real support, not just information.

⚡ Seek support now and check again after speaking to someone

Scoring Logic

Questions, scores, and triggers

Each answer carries a point value. The total determines the stage. Q6 (coping) is intentionally weighted higher — it is the strongest single predictor of need. Maximum possible score: 34.

Q1
Has the person you care for received a diagnosis or clear explanation?
No, we are still waiting0 pts
Yes, but I do not understand it yet1 pt
Yes, I understand the basics2 pts
Yes, and care needs are already changing4 pts
Q2
Do you see yourself as a carer?
No0 pts
Not sure1 pt
Yes, but I am new to it2 pts
Yes, I have been doing this for a while4 pts
Q3
How organised does the situation feel?
Chaotic1 pt +2 pressure pts
Some things are in place2 pts +1 pressure pt
Mostly organised3 pts
Stable but needs reviewing5 pts
Q4
What is your biggest pressure right now?
Understanding what is happening1 pt
Knowing what I should be doing2 pts
Managing appointments, medication, or tasks3 pts +1 pressure pt
Planning for the future4 pts
Feeling tired, stressed, or isolated5 pts +2 pressure pts
Q5 — also a safety trigger
Do you have support in place?
No2 pts +1 pressure pt
A little2 pts
Some family or friends3 pts
Professional or carer support is involved3 pts
I need urgent help5 pts 🔶 Urgent trigger Amber banner +3 pressure pts
Q6 — highest weight (intentional)
How are you coping personally?
I am coping fine0 pts
Some days are difficult3 pts +1 pressure pt
I feel overwhelmed6 pts Amber banner +2 pressure pts
I am exhausted or close to burnout8 pts Amber banner +3 pressure pts
Q7 — also a safety trigger
Is anyone at risk right now?
No0 pts
Not sure2 pts 🔶 Urgent trigger Amber banner +1 pressure pt
Yes, I am concerned3 pts 🔶 Urgent trigger Amber banner +2 pressure pts
ℹ️
Pressure level thresholds: 0–1 pts = Low · 2–3 pts = Moderate · 4+ pts = High. Urgent screen shown always produces High, regardless of points. Pressure score is derived only from Q3–Q7 (not Q1 or Q2).

V2 Content Layers

The 10 V2 enhancements

V2 adds condition-awareness, emotional depth, and professional support features on top of the core 6-stage engine. Each layer is independently editable via its own content file.

1
Condition Awareness Layer
Screen before questions · 8 condition options · session-scoped state
⌄

A new screen-condition screen is shown before the 7 questions. The carer selects the condition of the person they care for (or skips). The selection is held in _currentCondition = { id, label } for the session only — it is never stored in localStorage.

The condition drives condition-specific content in V2 Stages 2, 3, 6, and 8. Back on Q1 returns to the condition screen, not the landing screen. restartCheck() clears _currentCondition.

8 conditions Session state only Skip supported

Condition IDs (never rename — used as lookup keys): dementia · physical · mental-health · neurological · learning-disability · frailty · not-sure · other

2
What May Happen Next
Stage + condition-specific preparation bullets · safe language validated
⌄

Shows 3–5 bullets describing situations other carers at this stage have sometimes noticed. Provides preparation and reduces uncertainty. Each stage has a generic fallback plus condition-specific arrays.

Lookup pattern: NEXT_STAGE_GUIDANCE[stage][conditionId] ?? NEXT_STAGE_GUIDANCE[stage].generic

What You May Need to Do
Practical responsibilities · supportive tone · same structure as V2.2
⌄

Shows 3–5 bullets about practical responsibilities some carers at this stage begin to take on. Tone is supportive and preparatory — never demanding or guilt-inducing. Shown directly after "What may happen next".

Lookup pattern: RESPONSIBILITY_GUIDANCE[stage][conditionId] ?? RESPONSIBILITY_GUIDANCE[stage].generic

4
Condition-aware Resource Filtering
Directory + stage recs sorted by condition match · "Suggested for my situation" chip
⌄

Resources in the support directory can carry conditionTags, priority (1–3), and highlightReason fields. When a condition is selected, matched resources appear first with a green "Suggested for your situation" badge. Nothing is hidden — all services remain visible.

The stage-recommendations section on the result page also prepends condition-matched resources (up to 5 total). The directory gains a "Suggested for my situation" filter chip that shows only matched resources.

📄src/content/resources.js → conditionTags, priority fields per resource
Priority sort (1=specialist, 2=statutory, 3=general) Suggested chip · Safeguard note on filter-on
5
Pressure Check
"How you have been managing" · 3 levels · derived from Q3–Q7
⌄

Evaluates cumulative pressure from Q3–Q7 answers independently of the stage score. Returns 'low', 'moderate', or 'high'. If the urgent screen was shown, always returns 'high' regardless of answer pattern.

Pressure points: Q3 Chaotic +2 · Some things in place +1 · Q4 Feeling tired/stressed +2 · Managing appointments +1 · Q5 I need urgent help +3 · No +1 · Q6 Exhausted +3 · Overwhelmed +2 · Some days difficult +1 · Q7 Concerned +2 · Not sure +1. Thresholds: 0–1 = low · 2–3 = moderate · 4+ = high.

📄src/content/pressureGuidance.js → PRESSURE_GUIDANCE
getPressureLevel(answers, urgentWasShown) 3 variants (low / moderate / high)
6
Emotional Validation
"How you may be feeling" · stage + pressure + condition aware
⌄

A warm acknowledgement shown near the top of the result page, immediately after the condition display. Content is keyed by stage, then by pressure level, with optional condition-specific overrides. Never tells the carer how they feel — only acknowledges what is common at this combination.

Lookup: EMOTIONAL_VALIDATION[stage][conditionId]?.[pressureLevel] ?? EMOTIONAL_VALIDATION[stage].generic?.[pressureLevel]

📄src/content/emotionalValidation.js → EMOTIONAL_VALIDATION
6 stages × 3 pressure levels × optional condition override Triple-key lookup with generic fallback
7
Progression Reassurance
"Caring rarely follows a straight line" · snapshot framing · Stage 6 variant
⌄

A calm reassurance section positioned after the pressure check. Normalises moving between stages. For Stages 1–5 the section is titled "Caring rarely follows a straight line". For Stage 6, the title is "This stage is not a permanent state" — additional reassurance that crisis is not permanent.

Each variant: title, intro, points (4–5 bullets), reassurance (closing sentence). This section is intentionally not predictive — it never implies what stage will come next.

📄src/content/progressionGuidance.js → PROGRESSION_GUIDANCE
2 variants: generic (stages 1–5) + stage6
8
Condition-specific Service Suggestions
"Support that may fit your situation" · hidden if no condition set · national UK services
⌄

Shows 2–3 national service suggestions specific to the selected condition. Hidden entirely when no condition is set. Each entry has title, intro, and suggestions array. Each suggestion: name, type, why, optional url, phone, phoneLabel.

A footer link always points to "View all support" — this section complements the directory, it does not replace it.

9
Printable Carer Roadmap
Dedicated A4 print document · built from live state · not the result screen
⌄

The "Print Carer Roadmap" button calls renderPrintableRoadmap(), which builds a complete HTML document from all current state, injects it into #print-roadmap-section, applies body.printing-roadmap to hide all .screen elements, then calls window.print().

After print (or cancel), window.addEventListener('afterprint', ...) removes the class and clears the section. Ctrl+P without using the button still works via the existing @media print CSS — that path formats the result screen instead.

Roadmap sections: header, stage, emotional validation, progression, pressure summary, what may happen next, responsibilities, service suggestions, next steps, help links, Bridgit card (if applicable), urgent box (high pressure), review timing, footer disclaimer. No admin content ever included.

body.printing-roadmap CSS class afterprint cleanup Ctrl+P fallback still works
10
Conversation Prompts Expansion
96 prompts · 4 focus areas · session encouragement · professional mode only
⌄

In professional mode, the Discussion guide section gains a session focus selector: Opening · Practical · Support · Reflection. Each chip renders 4 stage-specific prompts from CONVERSATION_PROMPTS. Selecting the active chip deselects it, returning to the default FOLLOWUP_PROMPTS list.

_sessionFocus is session-only (not persisted). It resets when professional mode is toggled off. Session summary and print sheet include the selected focus and focus-specific prompts.

📄src/content/conversationPrompts.js → CONVERSATION_PROMPTS
6 stages × 4 focus areas × 4 prompts = 96 total Toggleable, session-only

Focus categories:

  • Opening — icebreaker and scene-setting questions
  • Practical — tasks, challenges, resources, finances
  • Support — networks, services, what is helping
  • Reflection — emotional processing, what matters, what would help

Professional Mode

What staff and volunteer mode adds

Professional mode is enabled via a toggle on the landing screen. It is session-only — never persisted to localStorage. It adds a structured conversation layer on top of the standard result.

🟢

Guidance banner

Green banner at top of result reminding staff to use the agreed step and follow-up prompts during the session.

✓

Agreed next step

Staff mark one of the 5 suggested steps as the agreed action before next contact. Stored in mcsc_agreed_step. Clears on new check.

💬

Discussion guide

Focus chips (Opening · Practical · Support · Reflection) render 4 stage-specific conversation prompts from CONVERSATION_PROMPTS.

💡

Session encouragement

A per-stage closing phrase appears at the bottom of the Discussion guide (e.g. Stage 5: "You do not have to manage this by yourself.").

📋

Session summary screen

Full-screen summary: stage, agreed step, steps, help links, selected focus + prompts. Copy button produces clipboard text with all fields.

🖨

Print conversation sheet

When printing from the session summary screen, an A4 conversation sheet appends: stage, agreed step, focus prompts, blank notes box.

ℹ️
CSS control: body.professional-mode class drives all show/hide logic for pro-only elements. Toggling professional mode adds/removes this class — no JS-side display changes needed.

How to launch publicly

  1. Complete all 10 pre-launch checks at [TOOL URL]/?mode=review
  2. Set contentStatus: "approved" and launchMode: "public" in branding.js
  3. Bump CACHE_VERSION in sw.js (currently mcsc-v31)
  4. Bump the ?v=N cache-buster on the branding.js script tag in index.html and match it in APP_SHELL in sw.js
  5. Deploy. Test on a real mobile device.
✅
To embed on a partner website: <iframe src="[URL]/?embed=true" width="100%" height="750" allow="clipboard-write"></iframe>. The embed=true parameter hides the meta-links row, version footer, and conversation toggle.

Data & Privacy

What is stored — and what is not

CareStage is built privacy-first. No personal data is collected, transmitted, or stored externally. All storage is anonymous and device-local only.

localStorage keyWhat it storesMax sizeCleared when
mcsc_last_result Most recent result: date, stage number, stage title, urgentShown Single object User clears from privacy section or "Clear all saved data"
mcsc_history Last 5 check-ins: date, stage number, stage title, urgentShown Array of 5 User clears check-in history
mcsc_action_plan Stage number + completion state per action ID (true/false + date) Per-stage object User resets action plan or clears all data
mcsc_reminder Reminder date (ISO string) + interval days Object or null Reminder fires or is dismissed
mcsc_stats Anonymous aggregate counts: checks started/completed, stage distribution, urgent shown, feedback yes/no Single object User clears all data from Insights
mcsc_feedback Array of { date, stage, helpful } — no personal data Array max 100 User clears all data from Insights
mcsc_agreed_step { stageNum, stepIndex, stepText } — professional mode agreed step Object or absent New check starts (restartCheck() clears it)
mcsc_launch_checklist Pre-launch checklist progress (boolean per item) Object Not auto-cleared — stays until manually removed

What is never stored

  • ✕ Names or contact details of any kind
  • ✕ Individual question answers (only the aggregate stage outcome)
  • ✕ Medical or care information
  • ✕ IP address or any device identifier
  • ✕ Anything that links a person to a check-in

Analytics

The logEvent() function writes to the browser console only. The updateStats() function writes anonymous aggregates to mcsc_stats.

No external analytics provider is connected. The Insights screen reads from mcsc_stats and mcsc_history locally. To add a real provider, replace or wrap the _origLogEvent call in index.html. Requires charity sign-off and privacy policy update.
